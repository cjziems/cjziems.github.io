I am a first-year PhD student in the School of Interactive Computing at Georgia Tech, where I am fortunate to be advised by Prof. Diyi Yang. I study the social implications of language on the web, including political orientation of text, framing effects, and anti-social behaviors like cyberbullying and hate speech. I am also interested in the ethical behavior of language models trained on web-scale language data. My research largely draws on methods from Computational Social Science and Natural Language Processing, and I actively collaborate with colleagues from Political Science, Public Policy, and Linguistics.
